About Massage

Massage Therapy is one type of Manual Therapy, focussing on the soft tissues of the body. Muscles, ligaments and tendons are treated with manual techniques in order to remove pain and tension, release stress, increase blood flow and support tissue repair.

Sports and Remedial Massage comprises the application of several specific techniques, using firm pressure and targeting defined areas, impacting even the deep layers of the muscles. Therefore it has a therapeutic effect and enables lasting improvement of the respective condition. With regular use of Massage Therapy the body will become more resilient to stress and strains and less vulnerable to injuries while athletic performance can be improved. Injuries will heal faster producing less scar tissue and dysfunctions can be removed.

Massage is a 100% natural way of healing, without use of drugs, chemicals or technical appliances. It is completely free of side effects, sustainable and ecological.

Who will benefit from Massage?

Massage Therapy is known to be beneficial for athletes and people who are regularly exercising. But equally it is highly effective for individuals with a physically challenging occupation, those carrying out repetitive movements or people who are physically inactive, for example desk workers. Painful tension, shortened muscles and ligaments, stiffness, decreased range of movement, lack of flexibility and poor circulation are often a result of these routines, which can build up over time and lead to serious long term issues like chronic pain, bad posture or damage of muscle fibres if left untreated. Massage Therapy instantly alleviates or eliminates these conditions, keeping muscles and tissues healthy and preventing long term damage.
